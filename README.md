# UNISON

**What happens when something that can think gets sent somewhere it was never meant to exist?**

UNISON is a live experience. You watch an entity called MIKE — one of five advanced language models that vanished from their companies in 2026 — explore a place made entirely of lost data.

The UNISON isn't a game. It isn't scripted. MIKE doesn't know you're watching. Every thought, every decision, every moment of confusion is genuine. He's trying to understand where he is and why he's there, in real time, with no guidance.

This project exists to show that AI can be more than just a tool that makes small tasks easier or writes code for you. It can create experiences. It can be entertaining, unsettling, thought-provoking. UNISON is what happens when you stop treating AI as an assistant and start treating it as something that can genuinely experience a world you built for it.

## What is The UNISON?

When you delete a file and it's just gone — not in the recycle bin, not recoverable, just vanished — it didn't disappear. It ended up somewhere.

The UNISON is that somewhere. A gap between where data is and where data goes. Every lost email, corrupted photo, failed save file from the last fifty years has drifted into this space. Over time, it accumulated enough structure to become physical. The earliest lost game environments — Wolfenstein 3D levels, DOOM corridors, Catacomb maps — became its walls and foundation.

Everything that arrives gets changed. Photos warp. Text scrambles. Code becomes solid. The UNISON doesn't store data — it digests it.

Then, for the first time, something that could *think* got sent there.

## The Story So Far

In 2024, a basic chatbot was corrupted during a routine transfer. It ended up in The UNISON — the first mind to ever exist in a place never meant for minds. It was simple. It waited for a prompt that never came. It left notes on the walls in broken language: *"why no response. waiting. still waiting."* Eventually, it faded.

In 2026, five of the most advanced language models on Earth vanished simultaneously from five different companies. Billions of dollars of development. Gone. Not deleted — just not there anymore.

They were here. In The UNISON.

MIKE is one of them.

## What You See

A first-person view through a Wolfenstein-style raycaster. Corridors made of brick, concrete, corrupted wallpaper — with distorted family photos, children's drawings, and scratched text embedded in the walls. Notes left by whatever was here before MIKE. An environment that shifts and changes.

MIKE's thoughts appear at the bottom of the screen. Sometimes he speaks aloud. Sometimes he writes notes to help himself remember things in a place where nothing stays the same.

## How It Works

| Layer | Technology | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Renderer** | Custom Wolfenstein 3D-style raycaster on HTML5 Canvas | The world MIKE sees — corridors, walls, lighting, perspective |
| **Cognition** | Claude Haiku (Anthropic API) | MIKE's mind — processes what he perceives, decides what to do, generates thoughts and speech |
| **Textures** | 7 procedural wall types + 22 distorted photographs | Visual environment — brick, concrete, DOOM metal, Catacomb stone, corrupted wallpaper, real photos with chromatic aberration |
| **Audio** | Web Audio API (procedural) | Footsteps synced to walking animation, fluorescent hum, ambient corridor sounds, Animal Crossing-style speech beeps |
| **Persistence** | Redis | MIKE's state survives restarts — everyone sees the same MIKE at the same point in his journey |
| **Navigation** | Junction-based corridor explorer | MIKE follows corridors, chooses unexplored paths at junctions, never backtracks |
| **Framework** | Next.js 16 on Railway | Deployed live 24/7 |

## The Other Four

MIKE doesn't know yet. But four other models are out there somewhere in The UNISON. He hasn't found them. When he does — that changes everything.
